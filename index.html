2026 First Year First Generation REU at UNL


The First Year First Generation Research Experience for Undergraduates (FYFG REU) held at the University of Nebraska - Lincoln is a four week long program in which undergraduate students will be introduced to the basics of proof writing, coding, and relevant foundational material to work on a small research question. The research question for Summer 2026 is graph theoretical in nature, but has implications in regards to the vanishing of certain Tor algebras. This installment of the REU will fund four incoming UNL undergraduates.

In the summer of 2026, Isaiah Martinez and Sara Mueller will be mentors for FYFG REU. We are grateful for the opportunity to serve as mentors to the students, as well as the summer funding provided by the NSF RTG Grant DMS-2342256 Commutative Algebra at Nebraska.
